Leisure

Iwakura Highlands is rich in nature and there are many things to see in the surrounding area. You can enjoy the magnificent scenery of Oze National Park, Mt. Hotaka, Mt. Nikko Shirane, Marunuma, and more. There are also facilities for various sports all year round, and there are endless ways to enjoy yourself, such as skiing, tennis, canoeing, rafting, paragliding, mountain climbing, and hiking. Please consider visiting us for your next vacation.

Oze

Oze is one of the world's greatest high-altitude marshes. You can enjoy the beautiful nature throughout the four seasons.
The full-scale Oze season begins in late May when the symbol of Oze, "Mizobasho" (the skunk cabbage), begins to bloom.
The mountains also begin to turn green, and alpine plants such as marsh marigolds and mizobasho bloom beautifully one after another.
In July, the day lilies bloom all over vast Oze Plain, signaling the arrival of summer. Many lovely flowers bloom, and you can see small birds and insects.
Autumn comes early to Oze, with the leaves starting to change color in late September. The rowan and other plants change color all at once, allowing you to see beautiful autumn leaves.

- Oze - Mount Shibutsu -

Ozegahara offers a magnificent view with the snow-melting Mt. Shibutsu in the background.

- Maru Marshlands -

Marunuma is a beautiful and fantastical lake formed by lava from Mt. Shirane.

- Mount Hotaka -

A sacred mountain with an elevation of 2,158m. Many flowers can be observed along the hiking trail.

- Mount Shirane -

Mount Shirane, the main peak of the Nikko volcanic group, stands on the border with Tochigi Prefecture.
